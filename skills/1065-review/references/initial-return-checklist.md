# Initial-Return Checklist (Year 1)

Run when the Initial Return box is checked on page 1, or the facts show this is the partnership's first filing year. First-year elections are made by *how the first return is filed* — many are irrevocable or expensive to fix late, so the review's job is confirming each decision was made deliberately and its statement is actually attached.

## Entity and classification

- **Entity classification** — Confirm the entity's default classification (domestic multi-member LLC → partnership) is what was intended; if a different classification was elected, Form 8832 was filed and the acceptance is in the file. If an intended election was missed, flag late-election relief (Rev. Proc. 2009-41) for the preparer — do not paper over it.
- **Header data** — Legal name, EIN (matches the IRS CP 575 notice), address, business code, formation date vs. return start date. The return period should begin on the date the partnership began business, not the formation date, if different — this also drives when startup costs stop being startup costs.
- **Initial Return box** — Actually checked.

## Tax year and accounting method

- **Tax year** — Calendar year, required year under §706(b) (majority-interest / principal-partners / least-aggregate-deferral), or a §444 election (Form 8716 filed; annual Form 8752 required-payment obligation noted).
- **Accounting method** — The method adopted on this return *is* the election. Confirm cash vs. accrual is permissible (§448: aggregate gross receipts, C-corp partner, tax-shelter/syndicate status — a partnership allocating losses to limited partners can be a "syndicate" forced onto accrual) and matches how the books are kept or the M-1 explains the difference.
- **Inventory method** — If inventories exist: method adopted (including the §471(c) small-taxpayer book method), LIFO requires Form 970 with this return.

## Election statements — confirm attached, not just intended

- **§195 / §709** — Startup and organizational costs: deemed election to deduct up to the statutory first-year amount with the remainder amortized over 180 months applies automatically, but confirm the amortization actually starts on the return and costs are split correctly between §195 (startup), §709 (organizational), and capitalizable items (syndication costs are neither deductible nor amortizable).
- **De minimis safe harbor** (Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f)) — Annual election statement attached if fixed-asset policy relies on it.
- **Bonus depreciation elect-out** — If slower depreciation was intended for any class, the election-out statement must be attached; silence means bonus applies.
- **§754** — Rarely relevant in year 1, but if property came in with built-in disparities or an interest already transferred, confirm the posture was considered.

## Capital, basis, and audit regime

- **Beginning capital accounts** — Must equal initial contributions (cash plus the tax basis of contributed property), with **no carryover balances** — a nonzero beginning balance on an initial return means either the box is wrong or the books imported another entity's history. Contributed property with FMV ≠ basis starts the §704(c) tracking obligation now (see `loss-limits-and-transactions.md` §4).
- **Partner basis schedules initiated** — Outside basis tracking should exist from day one; reconstructing it years later is the single most common source of unsupportable loss deductions.
- **Liability allocations** — Initial §752 allocation consistent with the partnership agreement's economic arrangement (deficit-restoration obligations, guarantees).
- **BBA/CPAR posture** — Eligible small partnerships may elect out under §6221(b) on a timely return (all-eligible-partner test); otherwise a partnership representative must be designated. Confirm the choice was deliberate, not a software default.

## First-year consistency checks

- Depreciation conventions (half-year vs. mid-quarter — a year-end asset spree triggers mid-quarter) and short-year depreciation if the first year is short.
- Guaranteed payments, if any, are supported by the partnership agreement in effect during year 1.
- State registrations and first-year state filings match the operating footprint — route to Preparer Questions per the federal-only scope constraint.
