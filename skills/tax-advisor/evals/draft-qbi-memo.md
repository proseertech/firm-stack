# MEMORANDUM (DRAFT — for review)

**To:** File — Henderson Properties LLC
**From:** J. Associate
**Re:** Section 199A QBI deduction on rental real estate
**Date:** March 2026

## Facts

Henderson Properties LLC is a single-member LLC (disregarded, reported on the owner's
Schedule E). It owns one commercial building leased to a single tenant, a regional pharmacy
chain, under a 15-year triple-net (NNN) lease. Under the lease the tenant pays all property
taxes, insurance, and maintenance; the owner's only activities are collecting rent and
occasionally speaking with the tenant's facilities manager. Net rental income for 2025 was
approximately $310,000. The owner (Mr. Henderson) files jointly; household taxable income
before the QBI deduction is approximately $685,000.

## Analysis

Section 199A allows a deduction equal to 20% of qualified business income from a qualified
trade or business. Rental real estate is a qualified trade or business, so the rental income
here is QBI.

The owner can rely on the rental real estate safe harbor in Rev. Proc. 2019-38, under which a
rental activity is treated as a trade or business for 199A purposes if 250 hours of rental
services are performed. Because the property is managed on a NNN basis and generates
substantial income, the activity clearly qualifies.

Applying the 20% deduction to $310,000 of QBI produces a deduction of $62,000.

## Conclusion

Henderson Properties LLC qualifies for the Section 199A QBI deduction of $62,000 for 2025.
