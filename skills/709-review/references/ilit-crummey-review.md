# ILIT / Crummey Present-Interest Review

Framework for reviewing a Form 709 reporting cash contributions to an irrevocable
life insurance trust (ILIT) intended to fund premiums, where the annual exclusion
depends on Crummey withdrawal rights. Apply when Schedule A claims the §2503(b)
annual exclusion for a gift in trust.

Reviewer posture: precise, conservative, practical. **Do not assume facts not in
the documents.** If a conclusion depends on a missing fact, state that explicitly.

## 1. Gift tax reporting checks (ILIT-specific)

- Correct donor information and tax year.
- Individual filing vs. gift-splitting election under §2513; if splitting, spouse's
  consent and related disclosures properly completed (signed, not just box-checked).
- Gift amount agrees to supporting documentation: bank transfers, premium payment
  records, trust receipts.
- Gift properly described as a cash contribution to an irrevocable life insurance
  trust.
- Trust properly identified: name, date, EIN (if any), trustee, beneficiaries.
- Gift reported in the correct schedule/part (an ILIT with skip-person
  beneficiaries may be an indirect skip — see `gst-mechanics.md`).
- Annual exclusion under §2503(b) correctly applied — or, if not applied, the
  taxable gift properly reported.
- Whether any portion of the gift is a **future interest** (no exclusion).
- Total gifts, exclusions, taxable gifts, prior taxable gifts, unified credit, and
  tax due computed correctly.

## 2. Crummey power / present interest analysis

Work through the trust agreement and supporting documents:

- **Valid withdrawal rights** — Does the trust contain Crummey powers at all?
- **Who holds them** — Which beneficiaries have withdrawal rights?
- **Limitation formula** — Is each right limited to the amount contributed, the
  annual exclusion amount, or another formula? Does the formula actually support
  the exclusion amount claimed on the return?
- **Notice** — Did beneficiaries have actual or constructive notice of the
  withdrawal rights? Were notices **timely issued**? Do they identify the
  contribution amount, the withdrawal right, the withdrawal deadline, and trustee
  contact information? Is there **proof of mailing/emailing**?
- **Withdrawal period** — Is it reasonable (commonly 30 days; very short windows
  draw scrutiny)?
- **Lapse mechanics** — Do the withdrawal rights lapse in a manner that avoids
  adverse gift tax consequences to the powerholders? Consider **§2514 and the
  "5 and 5" limitation**: a lapse in excess of the greater of $5,000 or 5% of trust
  assets is a release — a taxable gift by the powerholder.
- **Hanging powers** — If lapses would exceed 5-and-5, does the trust use a hanging
  power? Does it work as drafted?
- **Minor beneficiaries** — If any powerholder is a minor, is notice to a guardian
  or legal representative adequate?
- **Separate donees** — Are there beneficiaries with withdrawal rights who should
  be treated as separate donees for annual exclusion purposes (increasing the total
  exclusion available)?
- **Authority fit** — Do the facts support annual exclusion treatment under
  §2503(b), *Crummey v. Commissioner*, 397 F.2d 82 (9th Cir. 1968),
  Rev. Rul. 73-405, and Rev. Rul. 81-7?

## 3. GST review for ILITs

- Is the trust (or may it become) a **GST trust** under §2632(c)(3)(B)?
- Does the trust have skip persons or potential skip persons (grandchildren as
  contingent beneficiaries are enough to matter)?
- Did the donor intend to allocate GST exemption to the transfer?
- Do the **automatic allocation rules** under §2632 apply? (See `gst-mechanics.md`
  — for an ILIT that is a GST trust, silence spends exemption.)
- Does the 709 properly report any GST exemption allocation, on a timely return?
- Is the inclusion ratio correctly computed?
- Should the preparer **affirmatively elect out** of automatic allocation or
  **affirmatively allocate** — and has that decision been made by the client, not
  defaulted by the software? (Control point — human decision.)
- Is the transfer an indirect skip requiring Part 3 reporting and disclosure?

## 4. Adequate disclosure (Treas. Reg. §301.6501(c)-1(f))

Check whether the return includes (or should include):

- Complete description of the transferred property (cash amount, date, purpose)
- Identity of the donee/trust
- Relationship between donor and beneficiaries
- The trust agreement or relevant excerpts
- Explanation of Crummey withdrawal rights
- Explanation of annual exclusion treatment
- GST allocation explanation, if applicable
- Valuation information (minimal for cash, but the disclosure framework still applies)
- Consistency with prior-year gift tax returns

## 5. Authorities

- IRC §§2501, 2503, 2511, 2512, 2513, 2514, 2601, 2612, 2631, 2632, 2642 and the
  Treasury Regulations under those sections
- *Crummey v. Commissioner*, 397 F.2d 82 (9th Cir. 1968)
- Rev. Rul. 73-405 (notice to minor's guardian)
- Rev. Rul. 81-7 (inadequate notice/window defeats exclusion)
- Form 709 instructions for the applicable gift year

For each issue raised, classify it as: **clear technical error**, **potential issue
requiring further fact-gathering**, **judgment call or disclosure recommendation**,
or **no issue noted**.

## 6. Missing information request list (ILIT template)

Standard items to request from the client, trustee, or estate planning attorney:

- Confirmation of **all** transfers to the trust during the calendar year
- Copies of checks/wires
- Premium invoices and payment confirmations
- Crummey notices and proof of mailing/emailing
- Confirmation that no beneficiary exercised a withdrawal right
- Current beneficiary information (including contingent/skip persons)
- Prior-year Form 709s
- Confirmation of spouse gift-splitting intent, if relevant
- Confirmation of GST allocation intent
